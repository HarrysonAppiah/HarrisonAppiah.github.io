# 🔬 Overview

My research centers on the chemical transformation of municipal solid waste (MSW) and agroforestry residues into value-added materials and platform chemicals. By integrating principles of materials chemistry, reaction engineering, and green solvent systems, I design and optimize scalable processes for waste valorization. This work emphasizes mechanistic understanding, structure–property relationships, and process intensification to enable circular resource utilization.

---

## 🧪 Core Research Areas

### 1. Plastic Recovery from Municipal Solid Waste
- Solvent-based extraction of polyethylene (PE) and polypropylene (PP) from mixed waste streams  
- Comparative evaluation of aromatic solvents (e.g., xylene vs. toluene)  
- Process optimization for yield, selectivity, and purity  

---

### 2. Biochar and Carbon Materials
- Pyrolysis of biogenic MSW to produce functional carbon materials  
- Investigation of structural evolution, graphitization, and porosity  
- Applications in adsorption, energy storage, and environmental remediation  

---

### 3. Biomass Valorization and Green Solvents
- Conversion of lignocellulosic biomass (e.g., xylan) into platform chemicals such as furfural  
- Use of deep eutectic solvents (DES) as sustainable reaction media  
- Reaction kinetics, yield optimization, and solvent recyclability  

---

### 4. Sustainable Polymer Composites
- Development of biodegradable and recycled polymer composites  
- Processing into films, molded parts, and 3D printing filaments  
- Structure–property relationships and performance evaluation  

---

## ⚙️ Experimental Techniques

My work combines synthesis, processing, and advanced characterization:

- **Chemical Analysis:** GC-MS, GC, HPLC/UHPLC  
- **Thermal Analysis:** DSC, TGA, TMA, DMA  
- **Surface & Structural Analysis:** BET, FTIR, UV-Vis  
- **Processing Techniques:** Melt extrusion, injection molding, film casting, FDM 3D printing
